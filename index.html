CONEY island park

An ecologically sustainable park in Coney Island, also known as Pulau Serangoon, a small island off the northeastern coast of Singapore. With a plethora of things to do and places to explore, it is a must-visit place for anyone who loves hiking, animals or nature!

Things to do

Rent a bike

Cycle along the 2.5km Coney Island Park Connector, and enjoy the scenic view of Serangoon Reservoir with the wind blowing through your hair!

Go on a guided tour

Make friends with fellow nature enthusiasts and learn more about the history of Coney Island!

Explore Singapore's biodiversity

Immerse youself in nature and catch a glimpse of the wildlife busy at work in their natural habitat. 

Nature's Highlight

Oriental Whip Snake

Unlike most snakes, this bright green Oriental Whip Snake is an active hunter during the day , utilising its camouflage to wait for prey.

Estuary

The tidal mouth of a freshwater river that meets the seas, creating the right water conditions for mangrooves. Come view it at high tide!

Cycads

Locally rare in the wild, cycads usually grow very slowly and can live up to 1000 years old!

Fruit of the Simpoh Ayer

A bright red fruit of a pioneer local species of trees. Its large leaves were once used to wrap food in & its flowers do not have any fragrance or produce any nectar!

Tips for a safe & enjoyable trip

  • Dress comfortably and put on suitable footwear
  • Wear long pants & covered shoes, and apply insect repellent to protect yourself from insects
  • Wear a hat, put on sunglasses and apply sunscreen to shield yourself from the sun
  • Drink ample fluids to stay hydrated
  • Walk along the desired paths to protect the natural environment of the park.
  • Bag your trash & bring it off the island
  • Activities such as poaching, releasing and feeding of animals, damaging and removing plants & those that cause pollution are strictly prohibited
  • Clean up after your pets and leave them leashed
  • Camping is not allowed
  • Coney Island Park is closed from 7pm - 7am​
